Warmup Impact Validation — 2026-04-07
======================================

Test: Does a warmup run before timed iterations produce more stable results?

Setup:
  Site: books-toscrape (60 pages)
  Concurrency: 5
  Runs per condition: 4 timed iterations
  Tools tested: markcrawl (requests-based), crawl4ai (Playwright-based)
  Machine: macOS Darwin 25.3.0

Protocol:
  WITHOUT warmup: 4 iterations run cold (no prior crawl of the site)
  WITH warmup:    1 throwaway warmup run, then 4 timed iterations

Raw Results
-----------

=== markcrawl: WITHOUT warmup ===
  iter 1: 9.72s (60 pages, 6.2 p/s)
  iter 2: 8.71s (60 pages, 6.9 p/s)
  iter 3: 8.32s (60 pages, 7.2 p/s)
  iter 4: 9.07s (60 pages, 6.6 p/s)

=== markcrawl: WITH warmup ===
  warmup: 8.32s (discarded)
  iter 1: 7.72s (60 pages, 7.8 p/s)
  iter 2: 7.64s (60 pages, 7.8 p/s)
  iter 3: 7.96s (60 pages, 7.5 p/s)
  iter 4: 8.36s (60 pages, 7.2 p/s)

=== crawl4ai: WITHOUT warmup ===
  iter 1: 6.95s (60 pages, 8.6 p/s)
  iter 2: 5.86s (60 pages, 10.2 p/s)
  iter 3: 5.48s (60 pages, 11.0 p/s)
  iter 4: 5.34s (60 pages, 11.2 p/s)

=== crawl4ai: WITH warmup ===
  warmup: 5.60s (discarded)
  iter 1: 6.09s (60 pages, 9.8 p/s)
  iter 2: 5.98s (60 pages, 10.0 p/s)
  iter 3: 5.43s (60 pages, 11.0 p/s)
  iter 4: 5.28s (60 pages, 11.4 p/s)

Summary
-------

                      | Median  | Std dev | 1st iter | Range
  --------------------|---------|---------|----------|---------------
  markcrawl (cold)    | 8.89s   | 0.59s   | 9.72s    | 8.32 - 9.72s
  markcrawl (warm)    | 7.84s   | 0.32s   | 7.72s    | 7.64 - 8.36s
  crawl4ai  (cold)    | 5.67s   | 0.73s   | 6.95s    | 5.34 - 6.95s
  crawl4ai  (warm)    | 5.71s   | 0.40s   | 6.09s    | 5.28 - 6.09s

Key Metrics
-----------

  markcrawl:
    Median difference (cold vs warm):     13.3%  (warm is faster)
    Variance reduction with warmup:       46%    (stddev 0.59 → 0.32)
    Cold first iter vs warm median:       23.9%  (cold first is slower)

  crawl4ai:
    Median difference (cold vs warm):     0.7%   (negligible)
    Variance reduction with warmup:       47%    (stddev 0.73 → 0.40)
    Cold first iter vs warm median:       21.7%  (cold first is slower)

Conclusion
----------

Warmup produces measurably more stable results for both tool types:

1. Variance drops ~47% with warmup for both tools. Lower variance means
   fewer iterations are needed to get a reliable median.

2. The first cold iteration is 22-24% slower than the warmed median for
   both tools, likely due to DNS resolution, TCP connection establishment,
   server-side CDN cache warming, and Python import/JIT effects.

3. For markcrawl specifically, warmup also improves the median by 13%,
   suggesting connection reuse (HTTP keep-alive) plays a significant role.

4. With only 2 timed iterations (our default), a cold first-run outlier
   has outsized impact on the median. Warmup eliminates this bias.

Decision: keep warmup enabled by default. The cost (1 extra run per
tool-site pair) is justified by the ~47% reduction in measurement noise.
